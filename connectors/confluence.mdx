Connect Confluence to Airweave to make your team's wiki pages, documentation, and knowledge base searchable by AI agents.

## What Gets Synced

The Confluence connector syncs:

- **Spaces** - Workspace containers and their metadata
- **Pages** - Full HTML content from wiki pages
- **Blog Posts** - Published blog content
- **Comments** - Comments on pages and blog posts
- **Labels** - Tags and labels for organization
- **Attachments** - Files embedded in pages

## Authentication

This connector uses **OAuth 2.0** authentication. When you connect Confluence through the Airweave UI, you'll be prompted to authorize access to your Confluence site.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add Confluence Connection">
    In Airweave, navigate to your collection and click **Add Source** → **Confluence**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Authorize Access">
    Click **Connect** and authorize Airweave to access your Confluence Cloud instance.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start Sync">
    Airweave will begin syncing your Confluence content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Entities

### Confluence Spaces

Workspace containers that organize pages.

**Key fields:**
- Space ID, key, and name
- Description and status
- Homepage reference

### Confluence Pages

Wiki pages with full HTML content.

**Key fields:**
- Page ID and title
- HTML body content
- Version number
- Space association
- Created/updated timestamps

### Confluence Blog Posts

Blog entries published in Confluence.

**Key fields:**
- Post ID and title  
- HTML body content
- Space and author info
- Publication status

### Comments, Labels, and More

The connector also syncs comments, labels, databases, folders, whiteboards, and custom content types.
